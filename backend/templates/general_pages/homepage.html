Is Sports Betting Legal in Missouri?

In short, no. Efforts to legalize sports betting in Missouri have, unfortunately, stalled in the State Senate for the last several years.

Now, in the absence of any other strategy, it's up to us (that's all of us, Missouri Sports Bettors) to let our lawmakers know: We want them to legalize sports betting NOW. Enough is enough. You can help by:

  • Being vocal. It can be as easy as a brief comment on Twitter, or as intensive as hand-writing a letter to your Missouri legislators. Every little bit helps.
  • Spreading the word! Sports fans hang out with other sports fans. If your friends support the sports betting legalization effort, let them know to get involved by subscribing to the newsletter or following Let MO Play on Twitter.
  • Keep playing. Whether you make the trek to Illinois, Kansas, or one of the 5 other states bordering Missouri that have legalized sports betting, keep it up! Our lawmakers don't care about much, but if they see money leaving our state, maybe, just maybe, they'll change their tune.

Legal sports betting in Missouri could bring millions of tax dollars to the state

According to the American Gaming Association, sports betting generated a record $4.33 Billion in revenue in 2022. It remains to be seen what, exactly, the tax rate would be if Missouri were to legalize sports betting. However, published estimates consistently predict legalizing sports betting would generate $20-40 million in additional tax revenue for the state's education system.

  • 01 What have other states done?

    Colorado generated almost $44,000,000 in additional tax revenue in the first 3 years since legalizing sports betting in May of 2020.

  • 02 Who benefits from legalization?

    The answer is small businesses and residents. Local bars and restaurants will have access to an entirely new marketing stream, which could attract new customers.

  • 03 How much can we gain?

    As a demonstration of what sports betting can do for the local economy - New Jersey sportsbooks generated $82.2 million in revenue in the month of May, 2023. Ending prohibition will create jobs in a variety of sectors, including casinos, sportsbooks, and technology companies with the potential to create thousands of jobs in the state of Missouri.
