Betting on Education: How Sports Gambling Legalization Could Transform Missouri's Schools

  • Brett K.
  • April, 26th 2023

The landscape of sports betting in the United States has experienced a seismic shift in recent years, with the Supreme Court's landmark ruling in 2018 effectively overturning the federal ban on sports gambling. Since then, an increasing number of states have embraced the opportunity to legalize sports betting, recognizing its potential to stimulate economic growth, create jobs, and generate tax revenue. In Missouri, the state constitution mandates that gambling tax revenue be used to fund education. As Missouri lawmakers weigh the pros and cons of legalizing sports betting, it's worth examining the specific areas of need within the state's education system that could greatly benefit from the additional funds.

Missouri's Budget Surplus and Education System

Missouri lawmakers have been grappling with a substantial budget surplus, reaching over $6 billion in 2022. Despite this excess in the state budget, Missouri's education system continues to face significant challenges and lags behind in many areas, such as below-average performance on the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) and a widening achievement gap between students from different socioeconomic backgrounds. As legislators discuss how to allocate the surplus funds, investing in education should be a top priority.

Investing in Teachers and Closing the Achievement Gap

One pressing issue in Missouri's education system is the relatively low teacher salaries, which can lead to high teacher turnover and difficulty attracting and retaining top talent. According to a 2018 report by the National Council on Teacher Quality, Missouri ranked 42nd in the nation for average teacher salaries. Legalizing sports betting and allocating the tax revenue to education could provide much-needed funds to increase teacher salaries and improve retention rates. In turn, this would contribute to a more stable and experienced teaching force, ultimately benefiting students.

Missouri, like many states, faces a persistent achievement gap between students from different socioeconomic backgrounds. A 2019 analysis of data from the Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education revealed that this gap has continued to widen, with economically disadvantaged students performing significantly worse than their more affluent peers. By allocating sports betting tax revenue to targeted programs aimed at closing this gap, Missouri can work to ensure that all students, regardless of their background, have access to high-quality education and the resources needed to succeed. This could involve investing in early childhood education, after-school programs, and tutoring services, as well as providing additional support for students in under-resourced schools.

Modernizing Infrastructure and Expanding Support Services

Many schools in Missouri face challenges related to outdated infrastructure and a lack of modern technology. A 2018 report by the Associated Students of the University of Missouri found that many schools across the state are dealing with aging facilities, deferred maintenance, and insufficient technological resources. Investing in facility improvements and technology upgrades can create more conducive learning environments and ensure that students have access to the tools and resources they need to succeed in the 21st-century job market. The funds generated from sports betting tax revenue could be allocated to address these pressing needs, enabling schools to modernize their facilities and provide students with cutting-edge learning opportunities.

Missouri's schools are also grappling with the growing need for special education and mental health services. In recent years, there has been a significant increase in mental health issues among young people. According to a study published in the Journal of Abnormal Psychology in 2019, psychological distress, major depression, and suicidal thoughts among American adolescents and young adults increased substantially between 2005 and 2017. Moreover, a report from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) found that the prevalence of mental health disorders in children aged 3-17 rose between 2003 and 2011-2012. With the tax revenue generated from legalized sports betting, the state could invest in hiring more special education teachers, school counselors, and mental health professionals to better serve students with diverse needs. Enhanced support for students with disabilities and mental health challenges can significantly improve academic outcomes and overall well-being, both inside and outside the classroom.

Conclusion: A Golden Opportunity for Missouri's Education System

The debate surrounding sports betting legalization is complex and multifaceted, with valid arguments on both sides of the aisle. However, by examining the specific areas of need within Missouri's education system that could greatly benefit from additional funding, it becomes clear that legalizing sports betting has the potential to make a significant positive impact on education in the state. By investing in teachers, closing the achievement gap, modernizing infrastructure, and expanding support services, Missouri can create a more equitable and high-quality educational experience for all students. As lawmakers consider the future of sports betting in the state, they should not lose sight of this golden opportunity to transform Missouri's schools and improve the lives of countless students.
